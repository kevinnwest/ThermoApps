Thermodynamics & Equation of State

The Peng-Robinson Equation of State (PR-EoS) is a semi-empirical mathematical model developed in 1976. It maps the non-ideal behavior of real gases, vapor-liquid phase equilibrium, and supercritical conditions by adjusting the Ideal Gas Law:

$$P = \frac{R T}{V_m - b} - \frac{a(T)}{V_m^2 + 2bV_m - b^2}$$

What physical factors does PR-EoS correct?

  • Repulsive Co-volume factor (\(b\)): Molecules are not mathematical point particles; they occupy definite physical space. When compressed, the remaining free volume available is less than the box volume. This creates strong physical repulsive forces at short range.
  • Cohesive Attraction parameter (\(a(T)\)): Molecules exert intermolecular attractive forces (Van der Waals/London dispersion forces) at medium range, pulling neighbors back and lowering the net impact pressure on container boundaries.

Compressibility Factor (\(Z\)) Interpretation

  • \(Z = 1\) (Ideal behavior): The volume matches the ideal gas equation. Visually, particles move in straight paths, reflecting low pressures or precise force-balancing acts.
  • \(Z < 1\) (Attraction dominant): The measured volume is smaller than the ideal gas value. Intermolecular attractive forces are active and pull particles together into clusters.
  • \(Z > 1\) (Repulsion dominant): The measured volume is larger than the ideal gas prediction. Over-compression forces particles so close that their nuclear co-volume shields repel violently.
